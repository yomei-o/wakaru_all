The Neutron Is an Honest Witness

Proton decay couldn't be tried at 10³⁴ years. But the neutron decays in 880 s, and the 13.8-billion-year-old helium abundance delivers a verdict on c·t=constant — the face-value reading is rejected; the α-invariant gauge is not guilty but unobservable. Γ ∝ Q⁵ / ΔQ/Q ≲ 0.34%

Rotate i onto the Imaginary Axis and It Becomes Temperature

Turn the same i ninety degrees onto the imaginary axis of time (the Wick rotation): oscillation turns to decay, the quantum to heat, and the period of imaginary time becomes temperature. Overlay c·t=constant and the temperature drops as 1/t — linking to Episode 7's BBN (cosmic cooling). t → −iτ / τ = ℏ/k_BT

Episode 10interactive figures
Decay Is the Erasure of Information

A nucleon is one bit, "n or p." As the universe cools and updates can't keep up, the bit freezes and only irreversible erasure (= decay) remains. Landauer's principle (k_BT ln2 per bit) bridges information and energy, rewriting Episode 7's BBN freeze-out. erase 1 bit = k_BT ln2

The Neutrino Tells You Only the Difference

At last, the mass side. No one can measure the neutrino's absolute mass; only the squared difference Δm² is measurable — Bonus 2's "absolute values are bookkeeping, only the difference is physical," happening for real in experiment. Oscillation is Episode 9's phase of i; the seesaw origin of its lightness shakes hands with the GUT scale. only Δm² is measurable
